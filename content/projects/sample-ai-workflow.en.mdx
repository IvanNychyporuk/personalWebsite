## Context

This project was developed as part of the KIBILD program. The goal: build a reusable, tool-specific prompt system for generating a coherent series of AI images — a 10-shot autumn photo session storyboard set in a historic German old town.

The workflow covers the full pipeline: creative brief → shot list → prompt engineering → quality checks → delivery package.

## Creative Brief

**Theme & Setting** — Autumn walk through a cozy German Altstadt with half-timbered houses, cobblestone streets, and café windows.

**Mood** — Warm, natural, genuine connection. The couple enjoys the walk without posed gestures or dramatic staging.

**Palette & Styling** — Neutral autumn tones: camel, oat, denim, leather, wool, suede textures.

**Design principles:**
- The street as a leading line; shallow depth of field for subject separation; soft background geometry
- Continuous color grading: soft contrasts, warm neutrals, natural skin tones across all images
- One couple throughout — clothing stays consistent, connection shown through subtle signals (synchronised steps, brief glances, light shoulder touch)

## Visual & Technical Guidelines

**Lens & Depth**
- Full-frame aesthetic: ~35 mm (wide), ~50 mm (standard), ~85 mm (portrait)
- f/2–2.8 for subject/background separation; f/4 optional for more readable environment

**Motion & Light**
- 1/320 s base shutter; adjust for splash shots
- Soft daylight as key (overcast sky or low sun)
- Gentle contrast; no blown-out shadows; warm neutrals in highlights

**Texture priority:** Wool / knit / suede / leather; wet cobblestones after rain; window reflections for layering.

## AI Tool Integration

**Adobe Firefly** — Compact prompts; lead with subject, lighting, lens, mood. Always include a camera perspective tag (e.g. "eye level", "wide shot", "close-up", "over-the-shoulder"). Avoid overly long technical descriptions.

> Example: *"Autumn old-town street, couple mid-walk, casual glance; warm neutral palette; soft daylight, gentle contrast; full-frame look, ~35 mm, shallow depth of field; wide shot, converging lines, cobblestones and half-timbered houses; natural skin tones."*

**Nano Banana (JSON)** — Modular fields: `content`, `camera`, `light`, `composition`, `quality`, `negatives`. Specify numeric values explicitly (aperture, focal length, shutter). Minimise style adjectives.

**DALL-E** — Best for narrative scene prompts with context and character description.

## Character Continuity

Full-body frontal reference images per character were generated first and used as anchors across all shots. Locked parameters: age/build, hair length/colour/style, clothing palette (camel/oat/denim/leather), shoe style, coat cut, scarf/knit texture, accessories (watch/leather bracelet), and overall fit.

This prevents visual drift across the series and stabilises the colour and silhouette read.

## Deliverables

- 10-image storyboard set with captions and per-shot technical notes
- Two full-body character reference images (front view)
- Ready-to-use prompt package: Firefly prompts (with perspective tag), Nano Banana JSON blocks for 2–3 hero shots, DALL-E narrative prompts
- Watercolour master prompt

## Outcome

A repeatable end-to-end workflow: **Analysis → Prompts → Iteration → Quality check → Package.**

Key takeaways:
- Start with a compact style capsule: camera feel, lighting, palette, textures, negatives
- Use numbers instead of adjectives (focal length, aperture, shutter speed)
- Assign prompts to the right tool — each has different strengths
- Lock character references early; visual consistency across a series depends on it
